# OnRamps AI Lab 2026

## From Passion to Product: Building AI Solutions with Microsoft AI

Welcome to the OnRamps AI Lab 2026 repository.

This repository serves as the central hub for the OnRamps 2026 internship project, where students explore how artificial intelligence can be used to design meaningful, useful, and impactful solutions. Through research, experimentation, and hands-on development, students will identify real-world problems aligned with their interests and build AI-powered solutions using Microsoft technologies.

---

## Project Goals

Throughout the program, students will:

- Learn foundational concepts in Generative AI, Microsoft Copilot, and AI solutions.
- Explore real-world uses of AI and intelligent assistants.
- Research a problem aligned to a personal passion or area of interest.
- Design and prototype an AI-powered solution.
- Develop and refine AI behaviors, prompts, and workflows.
- Document their work and contribute to GitHub.
- Present their solution during a final showcase. 

---

## Technology Approach

Students are encouraged to explore a variety of Microsoft technologies when creating their solutions. Projects are not restricted to a specific platform or implementation approach. The primary objective is to solve a meaningful problem using Microsoft products, platforms, or Copilot capabilities.

Possible approaches may include:

- Microsoft 365 Copilot
- Copilot Studio
- Power Platform
- Azure AI services
- Integrations with external data or services

The focus of this project is problem solving and solution design rather than adherence to a specific technology stack.

## Internship Cohorts

This repository supports student projects from two internship programs participating in the OnRamps AI Lab 2026 experience.

### CRI Interns
College and Career Readiness Initiative (CRI) interns will complete the full OnRamps AI Lab project by researching artificial intelligence, identifying a real-world problem aligned with their interests, designing an AI-powered solution, building a prototype, documenting their work, and presenting their results during the final showcase.

### SYEP Interns
Summer Youth Employment Program (SYEP) interns will follow the same project framework, exploring how AI can be used to solve meaningful problems through research, solution design, prototyping, documentation, and presentation.

Both cohorts are encouraged to apply creativity, critical thinking, collaboration, and responsible AI practices throughout their project journey while learning how AI can be used to create positive impact.

---

## Project Timeline

### Week 1
**AI Foundations & Copilot Upskilling**

Learn foundational AI concepts, explore Microsoft Copilot, and build a simple AI solution. 

### Week 2
**AI & Agent Research**

Research Generative AI, Microsoft AI products, Copilot capabilities, and real-world use cases. 

### Week 3
**Problem Research & Ideation**

Identify a problem connected to a personal passion and define how AI could help solve it.

### Week 4
**Solution Development**

Build and test an AI solution using a selected Microsoft AI product (not limited to one product). 

### Week 5
**Refinement & Presentation Preparation**

Improve functionality, complete documentation, and prepare for the showcase. 

### Week 6
**Showcase & Delivery**

Present the final solution, demonstrate its impact, and share lessons learned.
